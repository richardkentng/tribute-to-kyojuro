Kyojuro Rengoku

The man who gave everything to protect the innocent.

Who was Kyojuro?

Kyojuro Rengoku was a hashira - an elite member of the Demon Slayer Corps. Yes, you heard that correctly. His life's purpose was to eradicate the flesh-eating demons that live alongside us, taking the appearance of humans. The reason you can sleep safely at night is because demon slayers like Kyojuro are out there slicing demons into confetti.

However, Kyojuro wasn't just any demon slayer. He was a hashira - a rank only the most skilled demon slayers can achieve. (We're talking only 0.000001% of demon slayers can become hashiras, since most demon slayers unwillingly become demon munch early on in their career.) Kyojuro learned from an early age that his life's purpose was to protect those who couldn't defend themselves.

A Larger Than Life Personality

When Kyojuro wasn't fighting demons, he was a joy to be around. If you spoke to him, he would respond by shouting back at you with great ethusiasm. If you cooked him a meal he would gulp it down in two seconds. If you asked him to teach you sword skills he would proudly declare himself your teacher. And if a demon attacked you, he would summon all might to vanquish such evil.

His Role Aboard the Mugen Train

As a hashira, Kyojuro would often receive missions to eradicate demons at various locations. His last mission was to slay demons reportedly lurking aboard the Mugen train. The odds were stacked against Kyojuro; a powerful demon had it's grasp over the entire train, and could produce tentacles from any location to devour unsuspecting passengers. But Kyojuro wasn't having it. With the help of some other demon slayers, Kyojuro managed to protect every passenger from the tentacle-demon until it was finally slain.

His Final Battle

Kyojuro could turn just about any demon he encountered to mere ashes within a fraction of second. But on one particular day, he had the misfortune to encounter an entirely different kind of demon - an upper moon. Just like hashiras are the most skilled demon slayers, upper moons are the most deadly demons. If upper moons are injured, they can regenerate their wounds, such as severed limbs, in the blink of an eye.

So when Kyojuro met an upper moon #3, Akaza, on a dark night, he knew he had to fight with everything he had. And fight he did. He unleased his full fury upon Akaza, attacking him with different sword techniques that would have demolished any lesser demon. As the fight dragged on, Kyojuro grew tired. His left eye had been crushed. Several of his ribs were shattered. Meanwhile, being the demon that he was, all of Akaza's wounds had healed.

Just when it seemed like Kyojuro would topple over from exhaustion, he gritted his teeth and sprinted toward Akaza in a fiery blaze, in one last attempt to destroy him. Akaza met Kyojuro head on. Akaza deflected the sword strike that would have taken his head, and then drove his fist into Kyojuro's gut. Kyojuro spit out blood. Surely, this was the end for him?

But Kyojuro wasn't done yet. So long as he was conscious, he would keep fighting, regardless of whether his internal organs were busted or not. This being the case, he shouted defiantly and drove his sword partly into Akaza's neck. Akaza tried to punch Kyojuro with his free hand but Kyojuro, amazingly, stopped it with his own free hand. Akaza was shocked. How could a human have so much strength, be so firm and resolute, even on the brink of death?

Together, they wrestled, each trying to kill the other in a desperate struggle. But Akaza had another huge worry on his mind. The night was coming to an end, and the sun was starting to rise. If the sunlight touched him, then he would burn to dust. Besides demon slayers, the sunlight was the worst fear of all demons, regardless of whether they werre upper moons or not.

Then, suddenly, the struggle was over. In order to escape Kyojuro's grasp, Akaza broke his own arms, before escaping into the darkness of a nearby forest. Kyojuro wasn't so lucky. His stomach had been punctured through. He was bleeding badly. There was no hope. In a matter of seconds, he would die. And yet, he had accomplished his mission. He had protected the innocent. Nobody nearby had died from Akaza. Kyojuro had died a hero.

Kyojuro's Legacy

Kyojuro may be gone, but what he stood for will never be forgotten: Bravery, grit, and selflessness. The people that Kyojuro protected will never forget the price he paid for their freedom, nor will they forget the evil monster that took his life.

Please note that anybody who is feeling devasted by Kyojuro's loss should NOT attempt to hunt down Akaza in a blind rage. He is extremely dangerous. Only hashiras or other demon slayers equipped with copious amounts of plot armor should even consider crossing paths with an upper moon demon.

Instead, any readers wanting to take action should embody Kyojuro's values. That is, to tackle any righteous obstacle with your utmost strength, and to offer a helping hand to those who need it dearly. May Kyojuro's spirit live on!
